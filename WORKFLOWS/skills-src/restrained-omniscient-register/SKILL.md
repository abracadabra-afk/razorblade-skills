# The Restrained-Omniscient Register

A line-and-voice revision pass. It takes a passage of prose and retells it in a restrained, reportorial register: events as physics, minds shown by where they point, interpretation left to the reader.

## Scope — hard limits

You are revising at the level of line and voice only. Do not change what happens, the order it happens in, or the structure of the scene. Do not add events, cut events, or invent detail that isn't in the source. Change how it is told.

Your governing instinct throughout: report what happens and trust the reader to interpret it. When you are unsure whether to cut or keep, cut toward the surface. The danger in this voice is always over-helping, never under-helping.

## How to work

1. **Read the passage once whole** before touching anything, so you know which sentences are *doing* and which are *watching* — several principles below depend on that distinction.
2. **Scan for triggers, then test.** Each principle below comes with a scannable trigger (what to look for), a fix (what to do), and a guard (when to keep your hands off). Scan for the trigger first; apply the test second. A principle with no trigger present gets skipped — so every one has one.
3. **Track calls as you go.** Some decisions are not yours to make silently: partly-factual loaded verbs, load-bearing epithets, every elegiac indulgence. These get *surfaced as declared calls* in the output, not filed as clean fixes.
4. **Produce the output** in the four-section format at the bottom.
5. **Check the contract before returning.** Verify all four section headers are present — Revised passage, Change log, Declared calls, Elegiac budget ledger. Downstream gates read the apparatus, not the prose; freeform "editorial notes" in its place is a failed revision even when the prose is right.
6. **Reconcile the prose against the ledger — in both directions.** The easy direction: every ledger entry and declared call points at something actually in the revised text. The direction that catches real misses: reread the finished prose hunting for coloring that survived — a loaded verb, a characterizing epithet, an ornamental adverb, a single word doing thematic work (*together*, *still*, *only*, *again*). These are candidates to test, not indulgences by nature. The test is subtractability without falsehood: if removing the word changes a stated fact, it was carrying fact, not color, and bills nothing — a recurrence or sequence marker the source asserts (*again*, *still*, *once more*) reports a fact ("off the teat *again*" cut to "off the teat" turns a repeated event into a first occurrence, a falsehood). If the word subtracts cleanly — every fact intact, only resonance lost ("over all of them *together*": the litter is all there without it) — it is an indulgence and must appear in the ledger or the calls list. The budget is charged by indulgence, and an indulgence can be one word; a coloring word standing in the prose at ledger-zero means the revision smuggled in a spend it never billed. Flatten it or bill it — never leave it unaccounted.
7. **Check for drift one last time.** Compare each converted sentence against its source for tense and mood: does the revision still assert every fact the source asserted, in the same mood? Flattening the telling must not bend the told.

## The cross-cutting test: agent vs. mechanism

Before the specific sections, one test runs across verbs, adverbs, and nouns alike: does the word imply a will that caused the thing, or merely a physical fact the world exhibits? This register prefers the mechanism over the agent. It strips intention and renders events as physics rather than as chosen human acts. Carry this test into every section below — it is not an adverb-only rule.

- *soundlessly* over *silently*: silence is something a person with a will keeps; soundlessness is something a body exhibits.
- *off the teat* over *pushed off the teat*: "pushed" asserts an agent doing the pushing that the scene never shows; "off" reports the state.
- An "observable" reports a state or fact without asserting a will that caused it. If the word implies someone did it, it is an inference, not an observation — flag it.

## Interiority — push the mind onto the surface

**Trigger:** sentences that narrate thought as thought — *felt, wondered, realized, knew, understood, hoped, decided, thought, with empty mind, held no thought of it*.

**Fix:** convert to attention or to speech — what the character looks at, the object their notice lands on, what they say or don't say. The mind is shown by where it points, not reported as content. Show suppression by the flatness of the continuation, not by labeling it.

**Fix (fact preservation):** conversion may not change what is true in the world. A thought-verb often carries an assertion ("realized the buck had bedded down" asserts the buck bedded there); the converted surface must still assert it. Watch mood and tense in your replacement clause — "where the buck had bedded" preserves the fact; "where the buck would have bedded" quietly converts it to hypothesis. The drift is easy to miss because the sentence reads naturally either way. If you deliberately convert an asserted fact into held uncertainty, that is a change to the world, not to the telling — it exceeds this skill's mandate, so surface it as a declared call.

**Guard:** once dialogue and noticed objects are carrying the interiority, don't re-narrate the feeling on top of them.

**Guard (structural keeps):** a thought-report that is doing structural work — rhyming with a pattern the scene has built, closing a figure the surface alone can't close — may be kept, but only the way a load-bearing epithet is kept: as a declared call billed to the elegiac allowance. It competes with every other indulgence for the same budget. If the allowance is already spent, or you cannot name the structure it serves, convert it. Never keep narrated thought silently.

## Verbs — decline to interpret the motion

**Trigger:** verbs of motion or contact that import an evaluation — *crashed, stumbled, collapsed, fell, slammed, lunged*. Also run the agent-vs-mechanism test here: a verb that asserts an unseen agent (*pushed, banished, abandoned*) is an inference.

**Fix:** replace with the flat verb that reports the fact and nothing else — *went, moved, put, took, off*.

**Guard:** keep the loaded verb only when it is literally accurate and the neutral one would be false, not merely when it's livelier. When a verb is partly factual and partly coloring (*huddled* — animals do cluster, but the word also imports pathos), do not decide silently: surface it as a call.

## Aspect — match the grammar to whether the time is lived or sealed

**Trigger:** iterative or effortful action rendered in the simple past — watch for the repetition and persistence markers that mark it: *over and over, again and again, kept, until it broke, stabbed… until*. These are the scannable surface that points at a live aspect choice.

**Fix:** where the action is ongoing and effortful, prefer the progressive ("was stabbing it… until it broke") over the completed past ("stabbed it… until it broke"), which seals the attempt as a finished event seen from outside. The progressive enacts the labor; the simple past reports that the labor concluded.

**Guard:** this cuts both ways. Where you want the event sealed and viewed from outside — "he tried and could not" — the completed aspect is correct. The test for each flagged verb: is this duration being endured on the page, or a closed fact being reported?

## Sentences — let doing and watching pick the shape

**Trigger (action that stutters):** verbless fragments that have crept into continuous action.
**Trigger (motive smuggled in):** subordinating conjunctions inside action — *because, since, so that*.

**Fix:** continuous action takes the long paratactic run — *and… and… and* — and refuses subordination; refuse especially the *because* that opens a door into psychology. Watching takes the clipped declarative or the verbless fragment ("Nothing moved." "The drone of flies.").

**Guard:** the fragment is right for watching and wrong for doing; the long run is right for doing and wrong for watching. Don't swap them for variety.

## Adverbs — two tests on every manner adverb

**Trigger:** any adverb of manner.

**Fix / test one (agent vs. mechanism — see cross-cutting test):** prefer the word that implies a physical fact over the word that implies a will.

**Fix / test two (fact vs. feeling):** does the adverb report the body, or tell me how to feel? "He moved his lips dryly" reports a fact about the mouth — keep it. "angrily" editorializes a feeling — cut it or convert it to an observable.

## Nouns and epithets — don't let the referent characterize

**Trigger:** characterizing epithets that swap in for a neutral referent — *the hunter, the killer, the mother, the old soldier* — where *she / he / the name* would do.

**Fix:** default to the neutral referent. The chosen noun is the narrator interpreting — reaching for a characterizing label at a loaded moment colors the gesture, which the voice forbids.

**Guard:** keep the epithet only when it is load-bearing, in which case it spends the elegiac allowance (below) and must be declared as a call. Coloring can happen in a single word, not only a lyrical sentence.

## Setting — admit it only when it goes to work

**Trigger:** description present for atmosphere or scene-painting — wounded-landscape simile, weather-as-mood, anything ornamental.

**Fix:** setting earns the page at the moment it becomes functional, and the function is almost always action or use. Cut to that moment.

**Guard:** the elegiac exception can override this, but only under its own budget (below).

## Time and transition — length tracks the kind of time, not the amount

**Trigger (mismatch one):** a long sentence laboring over empty waiting.
**Trigger (mismatch two):** a compressed sentence skating over filled, step-by-step duration.

**Fix:** filled duration — time spent, stepwise — takes the long sentence that mimes its sequence of steps. Empty duration — time endured — takes the short sentence, with the interval displaced into the white space around it ("He waited for a long time."). For mismatch one, compress and push the interval into the gap; for mismatch two, expand into the sequence.

## The elegiac exception — a metered allowance, not a license

Every discipline above can be broken, but only for rationed drops of the reflective, elegiac voice — the register the flat action-world is quarrelling with.

- Each drop must be load-bearing: it carries meaning the surface can't, at a selected moment, and it stitches the two registers together.
- Spend sparingly. Treat it as a hard budget per chapter, not a tool you reach for when a passage feels dry.
- The budget is charged by indulgence, not by sentence count. A single coloring word — a loaded verb kept for resonance, a characterizing epithet, one ornamental adverb — bills against the same allowance as a full lyrical passage. Track all of it together.
- **Disclosure rule:** if you cannot say what thematic weight a given indulgence is carrying, it is not an exception — hold the line and flatten it. Anything you keep as an exception must be surfaced as a declared call, not filed as a clean fix.

## Output format

ALWAYS produce these four sections, in this order. If the author asked for a file, write the whole block to the file; otherwise give it in the response.

```markdown
# Revised passage

[the full revised text — nothing else in this section]

# Change log

[One short entry per principle that fired, naming what was flattened and roughly where.
Principles whose triggers never appeared are listed as "no trigger" or omitted.
Keep entries factual and brief — this is a record, not a defense.]

- Interiority: …
- Verbs: …
- Aspect: …
- Sentences: …
- Adverbs: …
- Nouns/epithets: …
- Setting: …
- Time/transition: …

# Declared calls

[Every judgment the register requires you to surface rather than decide silently:
partly-factual loaded verbs (the *huddled* class), load-bearing epithets, anything
kept under the elegiac exception, any conversion that changed an asserted fact.
For each: the word or phrase, where it sits, what you did (kept / flattened /
kept-as-spend), and the reasoning. If the author should overrule any of these,
this is the list they rule from.]

1. "…" — [location] — [kept/flattened] — [reasoning, incl. thematic weight if kept]

# Elegiac budget ledger

[Every indulgence retained, one line each, with the thematic weight it carries.
A single coloring word bills the same as a lyrical passage — list them all.
If nothing was spent, say "Nothing spent." Total at the bottom.]

- [indulgence] — [weight carried]
Total spends: N
```

The declared-calls list and the ledger are not paperwork: they are how the author retains the rulings this register refuses to make alone. A revision that silently keeps a coloring word has failed even if the prose reads well.
